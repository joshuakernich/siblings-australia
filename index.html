Chapter 1

~ Lisa ~

Lisa loves her netball.

She’s just been shooting some goals in the backyard. And now she’s hastily taking her shoes off at the front door. She keeps falling over, leaving her sneakers in a tangled mess. But she’s getting the job done.

Lisa races into the kitchen and finds her mother, Katherine, helping her brother Andy have something to eat.

“Muuuuum… I’m on fire!”

Katherine doesn’t respond because she is preoccupied with Andy. He is fussing with his food and is very agitated.

Lisa quietly escapes to her room. The door clicks closed behind her.

What would you do?

  • Get on with it. Life is busy, after all.
  • Slip a love note under the door.
  • Play a game with Lisa.

Give Attention

Quality Time

It's not always easy. But spending quality time with your child is essential for their mental and physical health.

There's some super simple ways to spend time with your child:

  • Take a few minutes later in the evening
  • Play a game
  • Simply ask about her day

It may sometimes feel like the moment has passed. But it's never too late.

There’s a knock on the front door.

Andy races to answer it.

It’s Lisa’s friend, Phillipa. She’s come over for a playdate.

Andy grabs her hand and takes her into the loungeroom to show her toy cars. Phillipa is a bit confused and Lisa is embarrassed.

"I'm sorry", says Lisa.

Lisa finds Katherine in the kitchen.

‘Mum, Andy won’t leave Phillipa alone. I wish he wasn’t my brother!’

What would you say?

  • "Lisa, that’s not very nice. He is your brother."
  • "You're in for a smack-bottom young lady!"
  • "How dare you!"

Give Support

Persmission for Feelings

Siblings need to know they can share difficult feelings such as anger without judgement or blame. Acknowledgement of the struggles experienced can bring direct comfort in the moment, and is sometimes enough to bring emotional relief and reassurance
